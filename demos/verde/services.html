Our Services

Designed around
how you want to live.

Verde offers three distinct services for different stages of a garden project. Whether you are starting from scratch, working with an established garden, or simply looking for expert guidance at the beginning of a process, there is a route that fits your situation.

01

Full Garden Design

Complete Service

Our most comprehensive service — taking a garden from its current state to a fully resolved design, ready for construction and planting. This is the route for new gardens, complete transformations, and projects where the brief is genuinely open.

The full design service begins with a detailed site survey, followed by a series of consultations to develop a concept that responds to both the site and the client brief. We work through the design in stages — presenting concept drawings first, then refining toward a complete set of technical and planting drawings that your chosen contractor can build from directly.

Throughout the process, you will work with a single designer. Every design decision is explained and discussed. We do not present finished drawings as a fait accompli — the design develops in dialogue with you.

    What is included
  • Measured site survey and analysis
  • Initial concept design and site presentation
  • Developed design with materials specification
  • Full construction drawings for hard landscaping
  • Comprehensive planting plan with species list
  • Contractor introduction and tender support
  • On-site supervision during construction and planting
  • First-year growing season aftercare visit
02

Planting Design

Specialist Service

A focused planting consultancy for gardens where the structure and hard landscaping are established but the planting lacks coherence, seasonal interest, or character. Available independently or as a component of the full design service.

Many gardens have strong bones — good structure, decent paths, an honest sense of proportion — but planting that was installed without real thought and has slowly declined into a collection of unremarkable shrubs. Planting design addresses precisely that.

We visit the garden, assess what is worth keeping and what should be removed, and develop a phased planting scheme that brings genuine seasonal interest and natural character without the disruption and cost of a complete redesign. The result is a garden that feels intentional, considered, and entirely new — without looking as though it has been replanted.

    What is included
  • Garden assessment and planting audit
  • Recommendations for removal and retention
  • Detailed planting plan with species selection and sourcing notes
  • Phased planting schedule and maintenance guide
  • Contractor support for planting installation
  • Follow-up visit at the end of the first growing season
03

Landscape Consultation

Initial Guidance

A focused, paid consultation for clients at the very beginning of a project — before committing to a full brief or a full design fee. The right starting point if you want expert input to clarify your thinking before you go further.

A landscape consultation is a half-day visit to your property with one of our senior designers. We walk the garden together, discuss what is working and what is not, and explore the possibilities the site offers. At the end of the visit, you will have a clear sense of direction — what your garden needs, what is realistic within your budget, and what the next steps should be.

Some clients proceed to a full commission with us after a consultation. Others take the advice and work with a local contractor independently. Both are entirely valid outcomes — we are not using the consultation as a selling exercise. We are using it to give you genuinely useful guidance.

    What is included
  • Half-day site visit with a senior Verde designer
  • Verbal assessment and design direction
  • Written summary of recommendations and priorities
  • Budget guidance for any proposed works
  • Contractor recommendations if required

Our Approach to Design

We design gardens, not landscapes.

There is a distinction worth making. A landscape is concerned with the large-scale relationship between landform, planting, and views. A garden is concerned with the experience of being in a space — the texture underfoot, the height of the planting around you, the quality of light through a hedge at the end of the afternoon.

Verde is principally concerned with garden-making in the precise sense of that word. We design spaces that people inhabit, move through, and return to. The large-scale landscape decisions matter — but only insofar as they affect the experience of the garden at a human scale.
