About the Studio

Fifteen years of
doing one thing precisely.

Aura was founded in 2009 with a narrow brief: design and install bathrooms for private residential clients who will not accept a compromise on materials, finish, or process. That brief has not changed. The studio, the team, and the standard of work have grown — the focus has not.

Founded 2009

James Ellery started Aura because he believed bathroom design deserved to be treated as architecture.

James spent the first decade of his career as a specialist interior architect, working on residential projects for private clients across London. Bathrooms kept presenting the same problem — they were being designed as an afterthought, specified from a showroom catalogue, and installed by whoever was cheapest on the day.

The result was bathrooms that looked reasonable in a brochure and felt wrong in use — materials that did not age well, layouts that did not function, and a finish that revealed its cost within two years. Aura was founded to solve that problem with a different model: one studio, one standard, design and installation under the same roof, and a principal who is accountable for every decision.

How We Work

Four principles we do not compromise on.

01

One designer, start to finish

Every Aura project is led by a single designer from the initial consultation through to the final installation. There is no handover between departments, no account manager in the middle, and no dilution of responsibility. The person who designed your bathroom is the person responsible for how it turns out.

02

Specification before cost

We specify the right material for the design before we discuss pricing. If a project cannot be built to the standard the design requires within a client's budget, we say so clearly — and we discuss what the options are. We do not quietly downgrade materials to make a number work.

03

Installation to match the design

Aura works with a small, consistent team of specialist installers who have been trained to our specification standard. We do not use sub-contractors selected on price. The quality of the installation is treated as seriously as the quality of the design — because the two are inseparable in the finished room.

04

Transparency throughout

Every decision in the design and specification process is explained to the client before it is confirmed. Material sourcing, lead times, installation sequencing, and any changes to scope are communicated clearly and in writing. We do not present surprises at the end of a project that should have been discussed at the beginning.

The Studio

A small team by design.

Aura does not scale for the sake of volume. We take on a limited number of projects each year — the number that allows every project to receive the attention it deserves. These are the people responsible for that standard.

James Ellery

Founder & Principal Designer

James founded Aura in 2009 after a decade working as a specialist interior architect. He leads the design of every Aura project and is the primary point of contact for every client throughout the process. His background in residential architecture shapes the way Aura approaches bathroom design — spatial, material, and structural thinking applied to the most considered room in the house.

Clara Singh

Design Associate

Clara joined Aura in 2016 following positions at two of London's leading interior design practices. She specialises in material specification and lighting design, and leads the technical drawing process for every project. Her rigour in the preparation of specification documents is the reason Aura's installations proceed without ambiguity or delay.

Marcus Reid

Installation Director

Marcus manages Aura's installation programme and leads the trades team on site. He has worked with James since the founding of the studio and is responsible for ensuring that what has been designed and specified is built exactly as intended. His presence on site is one of the reasons Aura's finish holds its standard from the design stage through to handover.

The Showroom

Chelsea — where every consultation begins.

The Aura showroom in Chelsea is not a conventional display space. We do not keep stock or run promoted ranges. The showroom is where we meet clients to discuss briefs, review material samples from our suppliers, and make the early decisions that shape a project correctly.

All initial consultations take place in the showroom. Showroom visits are by appointment only — we do not accept walk-in enquiries. The appointment process ensures that every client who visits has our full attention for the duration of their consultation.
