About the Studio

Eighteen years of
considered work.

Hartley & Co was founded on the belief that a kitchen should be one of the most enduring investments a homeowner makes — and that endurance depends on decisions made with patience, knowledge, and genuine craft. That has not changed in eighteen years.

The Hartley studio — Marlborough, Wiltshire
Our Story

Founded on craft. Built on trust.

Edward Hartley opened the first Hartley studio in 2006 after a decade working as a furniture maker and architectural designer. His intention was specific: to fill a gap between the large showroom chains — with their emphasis on volume and speed — and the one-off artisan cabinetmakers who lacked the design resource to serve high-specification domestic projects.

The studio grew slowly and deliberately. We have never opened more than one location, never taken on more projects than we can design and supervise properly, and never compromised on the material specification we present to clients — even when cost pressure from the market might have suggested otherwise.

Today, Hartley & Co completes around forty commissions per year across the South of England. Every project is designed in-house, manufactured in our Wiltshire workshop, and installed by our own team.

How We Work

The principles behind every project.

These are not values written for a brochure. They are the things we have consistently prioritised over eighteen years — because we have seen, repeatedly, that they are what makes a bespoke kitchen project successful.

I

Listening Before Designing

The first thing we do on every project is listen. We ask about your routines, your household, the things your current kitchen fails to deliver, and the moments in the kitchen that matter to you. The brief that emerges from that conversation is what drives the design — not a predetermined style or a standard module configuration.

II

Honesty About Materials

Beautiful materials are not always practical materials. We are straightforward about the maintenance that certain finishes and stones require in a kitchen environment — and we will always present alternatives if we think a material is wrong for how you live. We would rather you love your kitchen in ten years than be disappointed in two.

III

The Same Standard Throughout

The attention we give to the elevation that faces the room is the same attention we give to the interior of every cabinet, the quality of every hinge, the finish on every shelf. Our clients rarely see those details — but we do, and the integrity of the whole depends on them.

IV

One Contact, Always

You will have a single point of contact at Hartley from the day you commission us to the day we hand over your kitchen. That person knows your project in detail, takes responsibility for its progress, and is available when you have a question. We do not transfer clients between departments or pass design decisions through multiple layers of staff.

The Team

The people behind the work.

Hartley & Co employs a small, permanent team of designers, craftspeople, and project managers. We do not use subcontractors for our design or installation work.

Edward Hartley

Founder & Lead Designer

Edward founded the studio in 2006 after ten years as an architectural furniture maker. He leads design on all senior commissions and oversees the creative standard across every project the studio undertakes.

Miriam Cole

Senior Designer

Miriam joined Hartley & Co in 2012 from a London architecture practice. Her background in residential interiors and her particular expertise in stone and material specification has shaped the studio's approach to contemporary commissions.

James Whitmore

Workshop & Production Manager

James has managed the Hartley workshop since 2009. His background is in traditional furniture-making, and he personally oversees the production of every kitchen that leaves the Wiltshire workshop — approving each piece before it is released for installation.
