How We Work

The garden as a natural extension of the home.

Good garden design begins with understanding the relationship between the house and its setting — the views from key rooms, the way the light moves through the day, the axis lines that the architecture creates without knowing it.

We spend time with every site before we put anything on paper. That understanding shapes everything that follows: the structure, the planting, the materials, and the way the garden grows into itself over the first years after completion.

The Design Process

From first conversation to completed garden.

1

Initial Consultation

We visit the site, walk the space with you, and begin a conversation about how you use and think about your garden. No charge, no commitment.

2

Survey & Concept

A detailed survey of the site, followed by an initial concept design showing structure, circulation, and the broad planting approach.

3

Developed Design

Fully detailed drawings, hard landscaping specifications, and a comprehensive planting plan — everything a contractor needs to build from.

4

Planting & Aftercare

On-site supervision during the planting phase, and seasonal aftercare visits during the first growing year to ensure everything establishes well.

Materials & Planting

Nothing that only looks right on the day it is planted.

We are particular about the materials we use in hard landscaping — natural stone, reclaimed brick, locally sourced gravel. Not because they are fashionable, but because they look increasingly right over time and sit honestly in the English countryside.

The same principle applies to planting. We favour species with natural grace — those that move in the wind, seed themselves into the right places, and develop a genuine character over years of growth rather than looking permanently tidy and lifeless.
