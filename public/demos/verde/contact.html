Begin a Project

Let us visit
and see the garden.

The first step is always a site visit. We come to you, walk the space, and listen. There is no charge for the initial consultation, and no commitment of any kind. We want to see whether what we do is genuinely right for your garden — and we would rather tell you honestly than take on a project that is not the right fit.

What to Expect

A conversation, not a sales call.

We do not visit with a prepared pitch or a standard package to sell. We visit to understand the site and to listen to what you want from it. The conversation that follows shapes everything — if we proceed, it shapes the brief. If we do not, it still leaves you with a clearer sense of what your garden needs.

Step 1 Initial site visit

One of our senior designers visits your property to walk the garden, discuss your brief, and make an honest assessment of the site. Typically one to one and a half hours.

Step 2 Written proposal

If we both wish to proceed, we send a written proposal within one week — setting out the agreed scope, the design fee, and the project timeline. No ambiguity, no hidden costs.

Step 3 Design begins

Once the proposal is accepted, the survey is completed and the design process begins. You will have one designer throughout — the same person you met at the initial visit.

A Note on How We Work

We take on a limited number of projects each year — deliberately.

Verde does not work at high volume. We complete a small number of commissions each year, and every one receives the same level of design attention and personal involvement. If our current schedule cannot accommodate your timeline, we will tell you at the first conversation — not after you have already committed to working with us.

We come to you

All initial consultations are conducted at your property. We do not ask clients to visit a showroom or a studio before we have seen the garden we are being asked to design.

One designer throughout

You will work with the same designer from the initial visit to the final planting supervision. We do not hand projects between staff members once a relationship has been established.

Honest at every stage

If we do not think a proposed approach is right for the site — or if the budget does not align with what the brief requires — we say so clearly and early, before the design process commits to a direction that will not deliver what the client wants.
